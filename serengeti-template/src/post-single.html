Best Time to Visit the Serengeti

Sarah Mitchell
Safari Guide
January 10, 2026
5 min read

The Serengeti offers incredible wildlife viewing year-round, but certain times of year provide truly spectacular experiences. Understanding the seasonal patterns of the great migration and wildlife behavior will help you plan the perfect safari adventure.

The Great Migration Calendar

The Great Migration, one of nature's most impressive spectacles, follows a predictable pattern throughout the year. This massive movement of over 1.5 million wildebeest, accompanied by hundreds of thousands of zebras and gazelles, creates unforgettable viewing opportunities.

December to March: Calving Season

From December to March, the wildebeest herds gather in the southern Serengeti for calving season. This is an extraordinary time to witness newborn wildlife and observe predators taking advantage of the vulnerable young animals. The southern plains come alive with activity as approximately 8,000 calves are born each day during peak season.

The weather during this period is generally warm with occasional afternoon showers. The landscape is lush and green, providing excellent photographic opportunities with dramatic skies and vibrant vegetation.

April to May: Green Season

The long rains arrive in April and May, transforming the Serengeti into a verdant paradise. While this is traditionally considered the "low season," it offers unique advantages for intrepid travelers. The landscape is at its most beautiful, with wildflowers in bloom and dramatic cloud formations.

"The green season is my favorite time in the Serengeti. Fewer vehicles mean more intimate wildlife encounters, and the photography opportunities are unparalleled." - Sarah Mitchell

June to July: River Crossings

As the dry season begins, the herds move north toward the Grumeti River. June and July offer the dramatic river crossings that the Serengeti is famous for. Watching thousands of wildebeest plunge into crocodile-infested waters is one of nature's most thrilling spectacles.

This is also an excellent time for general game viewing, as animals congregate around remaining water sources. The weather is cool and dry, making it comfortable for extended safari drives.

August to October: Northern Serengeti

The herds continue their journey north, reaching the Mara River in the northern Serengeti. August through October offers multiple opportunities to witness river crossings, as the herds move back and forth across the river in search of fresh grazing.

The weather is dry and warm, with excellent game viewing conditions. This is peak season, so expect higher rates and more visitors, but the spectacular wildlife action makes it worthwhile.

November: Short Rains

November brings the short rains, and the herds begin their return journey south. This transitional month offers good wildlife viewing with fewer crowds and lower rates. The landscape begins to green up again, and migratory birds return to the region.

Beyond the Migration

While the great migration is undoubtedly spectacular, the Serengeti offers exceptional wildlife viewing year-round. Resident animals including lions, leopards, elephants, and rhinos can be seen throughout the year. The central Seronera area is particularly good for predator sightings any time of year.

Planning Your Visit

When planning your Serengeti safari, consider what type of experience you're seeking. If witnessing the river crossings is a priority, aim for June through October. For calving season drama, December through March is ideal. For the best overall weather and fewer crowds, June through October in the northern Serengeti offers an excellent balance.

Remember that wildlife viewing is never guaranteed, but the Serengeti's abundant animal populations ensure memorable encounters regardless of when you visit. Each season offers its own unique advantages and unforgettable experiences.
